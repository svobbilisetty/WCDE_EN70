How to brew a frothy caffe latte using the Sharpson Coffee and Espresso Bar
A caffe latte is a perfect marriage of rich espresso and steamed milk. Watch our video to learn how to make an authentic caffe latte at home using your Sharpson Coffee and Espresso Bar.
Prepare the Machine

Fill the water reservoir at the top of the machine with cold water. Lock the group handle into the machine and turn on the power. Allow the pump to run for 15 seconds to clean and heat the machine.

Measure out the coffee for a single shot into the filter basket in the group handle. Use the tamper to compress the coffee. Make sure the rim of the group handle is free of coffee grounds. Lock the group handle into the machine.

Brew the Espresso shot

Set your cup beneath the spout. Turn on the power. The machine heats the water the appropriate temperature and then forces it through the coffee grounds.

Watch the color of the foam as the espresso enters the cup. The foam ("crema") should be a reddish-brown or caramel color. At the first sign that the foam is turning white, remove your cup.

Steam and froth the milk

Clean the steam arm by turning it on for 5 seconds.

Fill a steaming pitcher with 8 oz of milk. Submerge the steam arm in the steaming pitcher. Turn the steam arm on full and move the steaming pitcher up and down as the volume of milk increases. Stop when the steaming pitcher is hot to the touch.

Pour the frothy milk on top of the espresso shot. Rock the steaming pitcher gently back and fourth while pouring so that the froth comes out.

Enjoy!

Tip: Arabica beans are the best beans to use for espresso because they have a more subtle flavor. Darker roasts produce a sweeter espresso because the natural sugars in the beans have carmelized. The beans should be ground to the consistency of sugar.
Tip: For frothier lattes, use higher fat milk, if you can afford the calories.
Warning: Always ensure that the group handle is firmly locked into the machine before turning on the power. If it is not, pressurized steam can escape and cause burns.
